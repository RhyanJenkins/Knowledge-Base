Handheld Radios

Turn On:

Hold the top left knob down until radio turns on

Select Channel:

Use the channel up or down button to select your correct channel

Change Volume:

Turn the top left knob clockwise or counter-clockwise to turn to desired volume

Headphones (Optional):

Attach AUX headphones to port in mic piece of radio. Note, only one ear will receive any noise

Speaking on Radio:

Hold the button on the radio, or mic piece. Wait for the 3 high-pitched beeps, speak directly into mic piece, then release the button

If you hear long, deep tone: This means another person is trying to speak at the same time as you, or the radio is going in/out of range. Wait a few seconds until tone goes away and light on radio goes black. Then try again.
Tip: Wait a second or two before pressing the button to respond to a unit. This will eliminate possible overlaps.

Voice Techniques:

Focus on the Four!

Quality - The desired voice quality should display a positive impression, alertness, enthusiasm, confidence, calmness, businesslike approach and a readiness to serve. The voice should not sound unprofessional at any time, particularly when under stress. Proper enunciation and pronunciation, i.e., words spoken clearly and distinctly, greatly improve the ability of the receiver to copy the contents of a message and eliminate needless repetition.


Volume - The desired voice volume should not be too loud or too low. An excessively loud voice does not increase the volume; instead it may distort the signal and create a higher pitch. TMC operations staff must guard against the natural tendency to lower the volume of their voice as they speak. Normal conversational volume is normally sufficient.


Pitch - TMC operations staff should use moderate pitch level changes in their voice as they speak. A monotone voice is unacceptable. TMC operations staff should develop a pitch that is not too high or low. A high-pitched voice may improve by talking slower and a lesser volume. A low-pitched voice may be improved by talking louder and using distinct enunciation


Rate - Normal non-radio speech is between 80 to 100 words per minute. TMC operations staff must remember that the pace at which messages are delivered is important to understanding their contents. Generally, a speaking rate of 40 words per minute is preferred for copying purposes.
