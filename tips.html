Tips and Tricks for HERO Dispatching

Keep an open word document, notepad, or sticky note. On this document you can keep:

  • A list of all of your HERO units/truck numbers/route numbers
  • Your break times and DMS check times, as assigned to you
  • Write down the incoming transmissions from HERO
  • Write down a list of the abandoned vehicles you need you HERO routes to check on, for easy access
  • Record all phone calls you receive/make

HERO dispatching notes are easier to take on a separate document first, instead of trying to enter everything into the event as they come to you.

When a HERO unit calls in a transmission, first notate the unit calling. Then, type what he tells you.
Example “141-35s bf Wells Branch RS White Nis Alt AKF3856”

From there, you can open your event. Once you’ve input all that information, you can erase it and replace the note on the document with the IN.

Example “141-12-“

Then, whenever that specific unit calls in, you can just click next to their number and continue to take down the new information.

When you have multiple Lonestar events open, you can stagger the events on your desktop so you can easily access them when a unit calls in. To keep it even more organized, make sure the order of calls you have on your document mirror the order which you stagger the events.

When you have multiple events going on, this will help to keep you organized.

When first starting, if you are unfamiliar with what location belongs on what HERO route, an easy way to locate is to use the Google Map with the HERO Route filter in place. When you click on a blue line, it will tell you what route that location belongs to.

To learn how to open the google maintenance map, see Google Maintenance Map tab in the How-To's section.

You can also look at the list of HERO routes in the HERO Routes tab.
