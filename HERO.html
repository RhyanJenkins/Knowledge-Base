HERO services include:

  • Relocate disabled vehicles to safety (push or escort)
  • Remove minor collision from the roadway
  • Provide traffic and lane control at collision scenes
  • Remove debris from travel lanes
  • Change flat tires (if motorist has spare)
  • Give air to low tires
  • Provide gasoline (about 1 gallon only)
  • Provide water to an overheating engine
  • Perform minor vehicle repairs
  • Provide jump-start to dead batteries
  • Assist first responders at collision scenes
  • Provide drinking water and cell phone services to stranded motorists

The following is a dispatching script meant to be a guide when communicating with HERO. There are five essential pieces of information that should be included in general radio calls. Those are:

Main Highway IH-35, Mopac, US-183, US-290W, or SH-71
Direction Northbound, Southbound, Eastbound, Westbound
Cross Street Example: Slaughter Ln, Grand Ave Pkwy
Brief Vehicle description Color, Make, Model, Style (sedan, SUV, ect)
Lane Affected Right Shoulder, Lane 1, ect

Lanes are numbered from left to right. Only main lanes use numbers.

Example:
On a 3 lane highway, you have the left shoulder, the left lane will be lane 1, middle lane 2, right lane 3, then the right shoulder.

Dispatching Examples:

"Control to 130."
"Go ahead."
"IH-35 northbound before William Cannon Drive we have a disabled white Chevy Silverado in the right shoulder in need of a tire change."
"En route."
"10-4."

Other Examples:

"Loop 1 southbound at Enfield Rd we have a collision blocking the express lane and lane 1 of the general purpose lanes. AFD, APD, EMS on scene."

"SH-71 eastbound at Thomas Springs Rd we have reports or a ladder blocking lane 2. Not verified."

"US-183 northbound exit ramp to Loop 1 northbound, we have a disabled commercial vehicle with APD on scene. Unable to determine what lanes are currently affected."

"IH-35 southbound near St Johns Ave we have reports of a disabled silver SUV in the right lane. This event was called in by law enforcement, but they are not currently on scene and the event is not verified."
